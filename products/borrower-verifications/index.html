Undisclosed Debt Monitoring, reduce pipeline surprises from application to close

One of the most common causes of last-minute loan fallout is a borrower opening new credit, a credit card, a car loan, a personal loan, between application and closing. This new debt can push the DTI over the limit, trigger a condition, or disqualify the borrower entirely. And you often don't find out until the pre-close credit refresh.

ISC's Undisclosed Debt Monitoring watches your borrower's credit from the original application through closing, alerting you immediately when:

  • A new credit inquiry appears on any bureau file
  • A new account is opened at any bureau
  • A balance change occurs that materially affects DTI
  • A new public record appears (judgment, lien, collection)
Required by many investors: Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, and many private investors require evidence of pre-close debt monitoring on conventional loans. ISC's UDM service satisfies this requirement automatically.

ISC also offers mortgage fraud reports, including comprehensive fraud detection screening for loan files. Contact your ISC support team to discuss fraud reports or Undisclosed Debt Monitoring.
