What trended data actually means for your loans

A traditional credit report shows a single point in time. A trended credit report shows how your borrower's balances, payments, and utilization have moved over the past 24 months.

This matters because VantageScore 4.0 and FICO 10T weight behavioral trends heavily. A borrower who has been consistently paying down debt looks meaningfully different from one whose balances have been climbing, even if their point-in-time score is identical.

  • Declining balances may be viewed more favorably by trended scoring models
  • Rising balances may score lower under trended models even with on-time payments
  • Payment consistency over 24 months carries more weight than a single month's behavior
  • Credit trends may be an important consideration when evaluating potential score changes
ISC advantage: ISC reports have included trended data for years, so they are already formatted for VantageScore 4.0 where your investor supports it. No new vendor and no transition cost.

Mortgage Refresh Report, review credit changes before closing

The most common cause of last-minute loan fallout is a change in borrower credit after the original pull, a new account opened, a balance spike, a new inquiry from a competing lender. ISC's Mortgage Refresh Report catches these changes before they cost you a deal.

What the Refresh Report shows

  • Every new account opened since the original credit pull
  • Balance changes on existing accounts, increases and decreases
  • New credit inquiries from other lenders or creditors
  • New public records, judgments, liens, collections
  • Payment status changes since the original pull
Many investors, including Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, ask for a credit refresh within roughly 10 to 30 days of closing. ISC's Mortgage Refresh Report helps meet this need, and tri-merge reports are returned instantly. Order anytime.

When to order a Mortgage Refresh

  • Roughly 10 to 30 days before closing, per your investor guidelines
  • Before issuing a Clear to Close to verify no last-minute changes
  • When you suspect a borrower may have applied for credit during the process
  • When a rate lock is approaching and credit confirmation is needed

Credit freezes during a mortgage pull

When you try to pull credit and hit a freeze, the process stops until the borrower lifts it. ISC does not handle credit freeze or unfreeze requests. The borrower must contact each bureau directly, TransUnion 800-916-8800, Equifax 800-203-7843, Experian 888-397-3742. Once all three bureaus are unfrozen, the loan officer can contact ISC at 800-290-2801 or repull through the MeridianLink portal.

Frequently asked questions

How quickly do I receive a tri-merge report?

Hard pull tri-merge reports are returned instantly upon ordering. Soft pull prequal reports are returned instantly upon ordering. Note: ISC soft pull reports can also include trended data, ask your ISC support team for details.

Are ISC's reports already compatible with the new GSE scoring requirements?

Yes. ISC's opt-in dual-model report includes VantageScore 4.0 from all three bureaus alongside Classic FICO, formatted for the current GSE scoring options (Classic FICO and VantageScore 4.0). Contact your ISC support team to enable the opt-in on your account where your investor supports it.

What if my borrower's credit is frozen?

ISC does not handle credit freeze or unfreeze requests. The borrower must contact each bureau directly to lift the freeze, TransUnion 800-916-8800, Equifax 800-203-7843, Experian 888-397-3742. Once all three bureaus are unfrozen, notify your loan officer so they can repull through the MeridianLink portal or contact ISC at 800-290-2801.

Do I need a separate vendor for Mortgage Refresh reports?

No, ISC provides Mortgage Refresh reports on the same account as your original pull. One vendor, one invoice, one call.
