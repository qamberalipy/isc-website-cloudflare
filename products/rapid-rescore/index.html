What a rapid rescore is, and when it can help

A rapid rescore is an expedited process to update a borrower's credit report, and recalculate their score, based on newly verified information. Instead of waiting 30–60 days for a normal credit reporting cycle, ISC delivers updated scores in 2–5 business days.

The difference between a 679 and a 700 is often a single paid-off balance or a corrected error. The difference between closing on time and files stalling is often whether your credit vendor can execute that fix in days, not weeks.

Illustrative exampleA borrower comes in at 672. ISC identifies two high-utilization credit cards that may benefit from a paydown. With an optional credit simulation, the projected potential score could move into the low 700s if the balances are paid down. The rescore is submitted and an updated score is returned in a few business days. This is an illustrative example only. Results depend on the borrower's full credit profile and are not guaranteed.

The ISC rescore process, step by step

1

Planning call with your ISC support team

Your dedicated rep reviews the credit report with you and identifies the items that may impact the score, with an optional credit simulation: which balances to pay, which errors to dispute, which accounts to address. Every action is ranked by potential score outcomes before the rescore is processed.

2

Credit Simulation (optional add-on, additional fee)

Before executing the rescore, ISC can run a credit simulation to model the expected impact of each proposed action. Credit simulation is not included in the rescore fee and is offered as an optional add-on. When used, it helps you spend rescore fees on moves that are likely to move the needle for the scoring model your lender is using.

3

Documentation and bureau submission

ISC prepares and reviews all required documentation then submits the request to the relevant bureaus.

4

Bureau processing and verification

ISC works directly with Equifax, Experian, and/or TransUnion to expedite the update. We track the status and follow up on the correction to support a clean application the first time.

5

Updated scores returned, 2–5 business days

ISC will reach out to the credit bureau(s) to confirm the updates have been made. Once finished, a new report needs to be pulled to obtain results. If possible, ISC can help pull one or two bureaus on the original report to save additional costs and obtain the results from the rescore.

What can be rescored

Most common

Paid-off balance updates

Accounts paid off or paid down since the original report pulled, but not yet reflected in the bureau's data. One of the most common and impactful rescore actions.

High impact

Erroneous late payment removal

A late payment that doesn't belong on the report, with documentation from the creditor confirming on-time payment history.

Common

Collection accounts

Paid or settled collections that still show as open or derogatory, with documentation of settlement or payment in full.

Common

Balance corrections

Accounts showing an incorrect balance, higher than actual, suppressing the score through inflated utilization.

Specialty

Public record corrections

Tax liens or judgments that have been released or satisfied but still appear as active on the credit report.

Specialty

Account information errors

Incorrect account status, wrong credit limit, misreported account type, any inaccurate information suppressing the score.

Scoring model notes

As VantageScore 4.0 and FICO 10T roll out for mortgage underwriting, trended credit data plays a larger role in scoring. ISC supports VantageScore 4.0 where available. FICO 10T scoring models cannot currently be simulated by any credit simulation tool.

For deeper guidance on scoring model differences and rescore strategy, visit ISC Insights.

Rescore fees and cost context

Rapid rescore fees are often charged per bureau, per line item updated. A rescore updating one account at all three bureaus is charged as three separate line items. Your ISC support team will provide a specific quote before any fees are committed.

Experian permits consumer conference calls for verification in some cases; Equifax and TransUnion do not. Your ISC support team will advise when a conference call may be needed and walk your borrower through the process.

Documentation requirements

Most rescores require documentation, a balance letter, paid-off statement, or creditor letter confirming the correction. Documents must meet bureau requirements:

  • Must include company letterhead, account number, consumer name, and current account information
  • Must be dated within 5–7 days of submission
  • Must be in PDF format, screenshots, payment receipts, and bank statements are not accepted
  • Must come from the creditor listed on the credit report

In some cases, a rescore can be processed without documentation when the creditor supports automated balance verification. Your ISC support team will advise which path applies to your specific file. Note: if the bureau cannot verify the information with the creditor on a no-doc rescore, the account may be placed under a standard 30-day investigation rather than the expedited rescore timeline.

No-document rescore option

Documents can be difficult to obtain. ISC can process a no-document rescore to the bureaus. The consumer must call the creditor and allow the bureaus access into their accounts to verify the information in question. Your ISC support team will provide authorized caller information directly when a no-document rescore is initiated. Once authorization is in place, the bureau can verify the disputed information directly with the creditor. This path is useful when documentation cannot be obtained quickly.

Important: A rapid rescore updates verified information on a credit report. Score changes depend on the scoring model in use and are not guaranteed. Your ISC support team will discuss realistic expectations before any rescore fees are committed.

24-Hour rush rescores

When a file cannot wait 2–5 business days, ISC offers a 24-Hour rush rescore for time-sensitive situations, rate locks expiring, closing deadlines approaching, or files that need to move immediately.

Rush service is available based on order timing and bureau processing capacity. Call 800-290-2801 to discuss whether rush processing is the right option for your file.
